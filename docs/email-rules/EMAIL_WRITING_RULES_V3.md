# Email Writing Rules V3

Updated: 2026-06-16

Status: Active authoritative email-writing standard for Chitu Systems dealer
outreach.

## Objective

Generate dealer outreach emails that feel like genuine one-to-one business
development messages.

The goal is:

- High reply rate
- Natural human tone
- Dealer relevance
- Low spam risk
- Product-market fit positioning

Do not sound like marketing copy.

Do not sound like AI-generated sales content.

## Core Structure

Every email must follow this order:

1. Personalized Relevance
2. Product Introduction
3. Dealer-Relevant Benefits
4. Natural Sales Context
5. Soft Company Mention
6. Single Call To Action
7. Signature

## 1. Personalized Relevance

Start with why this dealer is being contacted.

Examples:

- `I noticed your catalog includes Elegoo and Anycubic resin printers, so I thought this might be relevant for your resin customer base.`
- `I saw that your store carries Bambu Lab products and related accessories, which is why I wanted to reach out.`

Never begin with:

- `Hope you're doing well.`
- `Let me introduce...`
- `We are a leading manufacturer...`

Avoid generic openings.

Do not invent company-specific facts.

## 2. Product Introduction

Use one short sentence under 20 words.

Example:

`Hoopat Resin Tank is a quick-release resin vat designed for users who regularly replace FEP film.`

## 3. Dealer-Relevant Benefits

Use concise bullets.

Maximum: four bullets.

Focus on practical dealer value. Never use hype language.

Example:

- Compatible with many popular Elegoo and Anycubic resin printers
- Quick-release structure for fast FEP replacement
- Reduces a typical 15-20 minute FEP change to about two minutes
- Helps reduce maintenance effort and printer downtime

## 4. Natural Sales Context

Explain why the product fits the dealer's catalog.

Example:

`It fits naturally alongside resin printers, FEP film, vats, and replacement parts already sold by many resin-focused dealers.`

Avoid exaggerated claims such as:

- Best-in-class
- Industry-leading
- Revolutionary
- Game-changing

## 5. Soft Company Mention

Use only one sentence.

Example:

`Chitu also develops practical accessories for both resin and FDM users.`

Do not include company history, factory details, or certifications unless they
are specifically relevant.

## 6. Single Call To Action

Use only one question.

Preferred CTAs:

- `Would you like dealer pricing, product specifications, and sample information?`
- `Would you be interested in compatibility details and dealer pricing?`
- `Would it be useful if I sent over product specifications and sample information?`

Never stack multiple questions.

Never pressure for meetings.

Never ask for calls in the first email.

## Hoopat Resin Tank Rules

This compatibility message is mandatory.

At least one of these lines must appear:

- `Compatible with many popular Elegoo and Anycubic resin printers`
- `Designed for compatibility with a wide range of popular resin printers`

Compatibility must appear before maintenance benefits.

Reason: compatibility is the primary dealer qualification factor.

## PlateCycler Rules

Focus on workflow automation.

Priority order:

1. Bambu user relevance
2. Build plate workflow
3. Time savings
4. Print farm efficiency

Avoid engineering language, technical jargon, and patent discussion.

## Filament Dryer Rules

Focus on:

- Print consistency
- Moisture control
- Compatibility with common filaments
- Accessory cross-sell opportunity

Avoid performance exaggeration.

## Spam Risk Reduction Rules

Never use:

- FREE
- LIMITED OFFER
- BUY NOW
- ACT NOW
- GUARANTEED
- BEST PRICE
- EXCLUSIVE DEAL

Never use excessive punctuation.

Bad:

- `Interested???`
- `Great opportunity!!!`

Good:

- `Would you like additional information?`

## Human Writing Rules

Every eight to ten emails:

- Slightly vary sentence structure.
- Slightly vary CTA wording.
- Slightly vary opening wording.

Avoid identical patterns across all emails.

Emails should feel individually written after reviewing the dealer's website.

## Tone

Write like a business development manager contacting a potentially relevant
dealer after reviewing their catalog.

Not:

- A marketing campaign
- An AI-generated sales sequence
- A cold-email automation platform

Target style:

- Professional
- Concise
- Natural
- Relevant
- Low-pressure
- Dealer-focused

## Control Boundary

These rules govern email writing only. They do not modify dealer data, product
assignments, rankings, scoring, contacts, or recipient fields.
