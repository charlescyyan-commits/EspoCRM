# Email Generator V3 Spec

Updated: 2026-06-15

Purpose: Outreach drafting specification for future email generators. V3 moves from product-category selling to problem-solution selling.

Do not send emails from this workflow. Drafts only.

## Required Source

All future outreach emails must reference:

- `PRODUCT_INTELLIGENCE_V2.md`
- `EMAIL_WRITING_RULES_V3.md`
- Approved manual fields from the current dealer review workbook
- Dealer website analysis and contact enrichment records
- Outreach eligibility output

## Required Email Structure

Every outreach email must follow:

1. Personalized relevance
2. Product introduction
3. Dealer-relevant benefits
4. Natural sales context
5. Soft company mention
6. Single call to action
7. Signature

Begin by explaining why the company is being contacted using a real website
observation, approved dealer evidence, or verified product-category signal. Do
not invent company-specific facts.

The product introduction must be one short sentence under 20 words. Benefit
bullets must be practical, dealer-relevant, and limited to four or fewer.

## Product-Specific Problem-Solution Guidance

### PlateCycler

Problem:

- Manual plate removal creates downtime.
- Operators become the bottleneck.
- Printers sit idle between jobs.

Solution:

- Automatic build plate cycling.
- Workflow-efficiency product for Bambu A1 Mini users, print farms, Etsy sellers, education labs, and small batch production.

Email language should reference:

- Bambu
- A1 Mini
- build plate workflow
- print farm
- repeat production
- continuous production

### Hoopat Resin Tank

Problem:

- Traditional FEP replacement requires removing and reinstalling many screws.
- Typical replacement time is 15-20 minutes.

Solution:

- Quick-release FEP replacement.
- Typical replacement time reduced to about 2 minutes.

Email language should reference:

- FEP replacement
- resin printer maintenance
- VAT / tank maintenance
- reduced downtime
- maintenance effort

### Filament Dryer

Problem:

- Most dryers treat all filament types the same.
- Engineering materials often require different drying temperatures.

Solution:

- Dual Independent Temperature Chambers.
- Premium drying and storage system for engineering materials and advanced users.

Email language should reference:

- engineering filament
- moisture-sensitive filament
- different drying temperatures
- filament storage
- print quality
- dual independent chambers

### LCD Screen

Problem:

- Resin printer users need replacement LCDs and repair parts as printers age.
- Maintenance categories such as FEP, VAT, ACF, and Mono LCD parts can drive repeat demand.

Solution:

- LCD replacement supply for resin printer maintenance and repair channels.

Email language should reference:

- replacement LCD
- resin printer maintenance
- repair parts
- Saturn
- Mars
- Mono LCD

### Heater

Problem:

- Low-temperature environments and engineering materials can make print conditions harder to control.

Solution:

- Enclosure heating support for better environmental control.

Email language should reference:

- enclosure heating
- low-temperature environments
- engineering materials

## Existing Partners

Existing partners must receive Relationship Expansion Emails.

Do not write generic cold outreach for existing partners.

Relationship Expansion Emails must:

- Acknowledge existing relationship context.
- Mention current cooperation products if known.
- Introduce only one adjacent product opportunity.
- Ask for feedback, not a meeting.

Example pattern:

`Since we are already working together on Product X, I wanted to ask whether Product Y would fit alongside the current range.`

## Email Style Rules

Target:

- Looks like a real dealer-development manager wrote it.
- Not AI.
- Not marketing.
- Not brochure copy.
- Not generic export sales language.

Length:

- 80-150 words for first email.
- Follow-ups should be shorter than Email 1.

Format:

- Plain text only.
- No links.
- No attachments.
- No images.
- No HTML formatting.
- Use two to four feature bullets when helpful.
- Do not force a fixed number of features.
- Keep application examples separate from product features.
- No company brochure language.

CTA:

- Use one question only.
- Dealer pricing, product specifications, compatibility details, and sample
  information are allowed when asked as a single low-pressure question.
- Never ask for meetings or calls in the first email.

## Subject Line Rules

Good:

- Quick question about LCD replacements
- Bambu A1 accessory fit?
- Resin tank category question
- Filament dryer product fit?

Bad:

- Business Cooperation
- Partnership Opportunity
- New Product Introduction
- New Product Launch
- Revenue Opportunity
- Dealer Opportunity
- Available Now
- Distribution Opportunity
- Urgent
- Limited Time
- Free

Subject should feel like a real business question.

## Sequence Structure

Before writing the sequence, assign:

- `Primary_Product`
- `Secondary_Product`
- `Third_Product`

`Best_First_Product` is the starting point for `Primary_Product`. It must not
cause all three emails to repeat the same product.

### Email 1

Observation → Customer Problem → Product Solution → One Question → Close

### Email 2

Timing:

- 5 days later

Content:

- Reference previous email.
- Add one new value point.
- Ask one simple question.
- Shorter than Email 1.

### Email 3

Timing:

- 12 days later

Content:

- Soft close.
- No pressure.
- Leave door open.

## Multi-Product Outreach Rule

Multi-product outreach is the default unless the task explicitly requires a
single-product sequence.

- Email 1 focuses on `Primary_Product`.
- Email 2 focuses on `Secondary_Product`.
- Email 3 focuses on `Third_Product`.
- Each email has one main product problem and solution.
- Each email may briefly mention the other relevant product opportunities.
- Do not repeat the same product as the main focus in all follow-ups.
- Do not over-optimize the sequence around `Best_First_Product`.
- Respect `Do_Not_Pitch`, approved human selections, evidence, relationship
  context, and product eligibility rules.

Default rotation:

1. PlateCycler
2. Resin Tank
3. Filament Dryer

Dealer-specific rotation:

| Dealer profile | Primary | Secondary | Third |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bambu-heavy | PlateCycler | Filament Dryer | Resin Tank |
| Resin-heavy | Resin Tank | PlateCycler | Filament Dryer |
| FDM / filament-heavy | Filament Dryer | PlateCycler | Resin Tank |
| Broad multi-brand | PlateCycler | Resin Tank | Filament Dryer |
| Unclear | General Partnership | PlateCycler | Resin Tank or Filament Dryer, based on available signals |

Every email should naturally convey that Chitu Systems regularly develops
practical accessories and workflow-enhancement products for both resin and FDM
3D printing users. Rephrase this idea when the literal sentence would sound
mechanical.

Approved positioning directions:

- `Chitu also develops practical accessories for both resin and FDM users.`
- `This is one of several practical accessories Chitu has developed for active resin and FDM users.`

## V3 Writing Addendum

`EMAIL_WRITING_RULES_V3.md` is authoritative for future generation.

- Hoopat Resin Tank emails must include compatibility before maintenance
  benefits: `Compatible with many popular Elegoo and Anycubic resin printers`
  or `Designed for compatibility with a wide range of popular resin printers`.
- PlateCycler emails prioritize Bambu relevance, build plate workflow, time
  savings, and print farm efficiency.
- Filament Dryer emails focus on print consistency, moisture control, common
  filament compatibility, and accessory cross-sell.
- Vary openings, CTAs, and sentence structure every eight to ten emails.

## V3 Quality Target

Primary goal:

- Generate replies.

Not the goal:

- Immediate sales conversion.
- Meeting booking as the first ask.
- Broad product pitching.

The email should feel like a professional product recommendation, not a sales
pitch, dealer recruitment message, or partnership proposal.
